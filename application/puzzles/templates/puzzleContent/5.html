Splitting a Wooden Plank


There is a wooden plank of length n (n <= 50000 is guaranteed to be a multiple of 2) and we want to split
the wooden plank between two people, Alice and Bob, such that each of them receives the same amount of wood.
We can chop the wooden plank at any of n-1 evenly spaced intervals, but due to the different hardness of the wood at different points, each location will have a different cost. Find the minimum cost required to split the wooden plank equally between Alice and Bob.

Input:

The first line of input has one integer, n

The next line has n-1 integers, each , where the i-th integer represents the cost of splitting the wood at the i-th point

It is guaranteed that the answer does not exceed 2147483647.

Example:

Input:

6

5 2 10 1 3

Output:

5

Explanation:

We can split the wood at the positions costing 2 and 3

- - | - - - | -

Then give the first segment of length 2 and the third segment of length 1 to Alice and give the second segment of length 3 to Bob

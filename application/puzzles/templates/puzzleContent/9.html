Choosing Items

You are given N items. Each item has a cost that is a positive integer.

You are also given M outfits, each consisting of several of the items and has a niceness that is a positive integer.

You decide to buy some of the items, paying a total cost which is the sum of the costs of each item. We say you own an outfit, if you have bought all the items in it.
The total niceness you get is the sum of the niceness of each outfit you own.

The satisfaction you get from buying a subset of items is the total niceness minus the total cost.

Your task is to find a subset of items that maximize the satisfaction.

Constraints:

1 <= N <= 5000

1 <= M <= 5000

1 <= costs <= 109

1 <= niceness <= 109

2 <= number of items in an outfit <= 8

Input:

On the first line of the input are the numbers N and M (in that order).

On the second line there are N numbers which are the costs of the items.

On each of the next M lines you are given an outfit in the following format:

  • The first number is the niceness of the outfit

  • The second number is the number of items in the outfit

  • The rest of the numbers are the indices of items in the outfit (note we are counting from 1)

Output:

On the first line of the output, you need to print two numbers - the maximum satisfaction you found and the number of items you have bought.

On the second line you need to print the indices of items you bought, in increasing order.

Note that you can buy each item only once.

Examples:

Input:                        Output:

6 3                              5 4

1 4 7 8 666 4              1 2 3 4

15 3 1 3 2

10 3 1 3 4

100 3 5 6 4
